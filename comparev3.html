Preventative Security Model

IGEL’s Preventative Security Model centers on isolation, least functionality, and a locked-down, read-only operating system. By removing local data and minimizing the attack surface, endpoints become a hardened conduit to SaaS, DaaS, and VDI resources. Policy-driven access with strong identity ensures the right user, device, and context before any session begins. This approach reduces patching urgency, streamlines audits, and improves resilience against ransomware and supply-chain risk. With a modular, verifiable OS image and an ecosystem of certified integrations, organizations can align to zero-trust principles while maintaining a consistent user experience across locations and device types—without the overhead of traditional endpoint stacks.

  • Immutable, read-only OS with Secure Boot
  • No local data; SaaS/DaaS/VDI first access
  • Policy enforcement & identity-aware launch
  • Attack surface minimized; fewer agents
  • IGEL Ready integrations for layered controls

Continuity & Recovery

When the unexpected happens, business continuity depends on clean-boot options that bypass compromised systems and restore productive access fast. IGEL provides layered recovery modes including USB boot (UD Pocket), Dual Boot, and a Managed Hypervisor option for local containerized workspaces. These modes enable secure reconnection to essential services—communications, EHR/EMR, trading platforms, or critical line-of-business apps—without data persistence on the device. Centralized policies ensure only validated users and devices launch sessions, while UMS can re-provision or rotate images at scale. The result is a predictable, low-risk path to recovery that keeps operations running and reduces incident costs.

  • UD Pocket: clean boot on any compatible x86-64
  • Dual Boot: protected IGEL partition alongside OS
  • Managed Hypervisor: local, policy-controlled VM
  • Rapid reconnect to core apps and comms
  • Central orchestration through UMS

Delivery Flexibility

Modern work mixes delivery methods: full VDI/DaaS desktops, secure enterprise browsers, progressive web apps, and selected local Linux equivalents for offline or edge use. IGEL’s Adaptive Secure Desktop orchestrates these choices behind a consistent launch experience. Admins assemble role-based workspaces that route users to the optimal delivery path based on identity, posture, location, and risk. The result is fewer images to maintain, lower bandwidth demands, and faster onboarding across devices from OEM partners. Users gain a reliable, low-latency path into their apps with minimal learning curve—whether they’re clinicians on shared workstations, advisors in branches, or agents in contact centers.

  • VDI/DaaS, PWA, Enterprise Browser, Local Linux apps
  • Context-aware routing and policy selection
  • Reduced image & agent sprawl
  • Consistent UX across certified devices
  • Great fit for healthcare, finance, retail, and public sector

Manageability & TCO

Universal Management Suite (UMS) gives IT precise, at-scale control of endpoints—from golden image governance to context-based policy, firmware updates, and app entitlements. Lifecycle-extending repurpose use cases and agentless security reduce support effort and license overhead, while remote troubleshooting tools accelerate resolution. Sustainability goals benefit from longer device life and lower power profiles. Ecosystem integrations simplify identity, SASE, and observability, ensuring signals flow to the systems teams already use. Together these advantages drive a lower, more predictable total cost of ownership without compromising security or the end-user experience.

  • Granular policy & profile control via UMS
  • Repurpose hardware to cut refresh costs
  • Agentless model reduces complexity
  • Energy & carbon savings from efficient endpoints
  • Broad ecosystem & OEM partnerships
