IGEL (Baseline)

What to lead with

  • Preventative model: remove the endpoint attack surface (immutable OS, no local data) instead of chasing patches.
  • Continuity without external DR tools: UD Pocket / Dual Boot / Managed Hypervisor.
  • Delivery flexibility: covers virtual, browser, and local Linux app equivalents.
  • Large ecosystem (IGEL Ready), certified device options (HP/Lenovo/LG).
  • TCO: extend device life, agentless security posture, lower energy footprint.
Unicon (eLux & Scout)

How to frame the alternative

  • Purpose-built for Citrix; write-protected Linux and centralized management (Scout).
  • Strong for boot-to-VDI use cases; emphasizes repurposing Windows PCs.
  • Continuity mainly via recovery USB and reimaging flows; no layered recovery modes equivalent to IGEL’s full set.
  • Ecosystem breadth (device & ISV) narrower vs. IGEL Ready catalogue.

Differentiator Callouts (Use in Discovery)

  • Layered recovery (USB, Dual Boot, Managed Hypervisor) — clean-boot in minutes to resume collaboration tools while infra is remediated.
  • Preventative Security Architecture — immutable OS state, no local data, secure boot, MFA/SSO; shrink attack surface vs. agent sprawl.
  • Adaptive Secure Desktop — role/context-based access across DaaS, browser, PWA, and local Linux equivalents.
  • Ecosystem leverage — broad IGEL Ready catalog and multiple certified device OEMs.

Gap Watchlist (Monitor & Counter)

  • Citrix bundling: Unicon now packaged inside Citrix motions; accounts might perceive it as the “default.” Counter with multi-vendor flexibility + non-Citrix use cases.
  • Boot-to-VDI narratives: If the buyer is 100% CVAD, Unicon’s simplicity resonates. Reframe to IGEL’s five delivery models and continuity.
  • “Write-protected is the same”: Clarify that IGEL’s no local data + recovery modes are operationally different from simple write-protection.

Vertical Snapshots

  • Healthcare: tap-in SSO, shared stations, no-data-left-behind, fast clean-boot during ransomware events.
  • Financial Services: strong MFA/SSO posture, least-privilege desktops, browser-isolation patterns.
  • Government: immutable state & centralized policy for compliance, offline-resilient device posture.
  • Manufacturing/Retail: kiosk & floor devices, PWA models, minimal downtime via UD Pocket.

Qualification (MEDDPICC-style prompts)

  • Metrics: device refresh cycle? ransomware MTTR? % of SaaS vs VDI?
  • Economic buyer: CISO vs EUC leader — who owns endpoint risk?
  • Pain: patch velocity, incident reimaging time, hybrid app mix.
  • Paper process: security review for “no local data” and immutable OS?

Common Objections & Reframes

  • “Write-protected equals secure.” → Not if credentials/apps still persist. IGEL enforces no local data and immutability with recovery modes.
  • “We’re all-in on Citrix.” → Great; IGEL supports CVAD and broadens to browser & local Linux workloads + continuity.
  • “We can just reimage.” → That is downtime. IGEL clean-boots in minutes; users work while IT fixes root cause.

Battlecard One-Liners

  • Security: Prevent, don’t chase — immutable OS + no local data.
  • Continuity: USB / Dual Boot / Managed Hypervisor.
  • Flexibility: VDI • Browser • PWA • Local Linux equiv.
  • Ecosystem: IGEL Ready integrations & certified hardware.
