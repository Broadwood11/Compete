Stage 1 — Define the Evaluation Framework

We anchor the comparison in IGEL’s Preventative Security Model and Adaptive Secure Desktop, scoring across seven dimensions that matter for secure, flexible endpoint delivery.

  • Security & Zero Trust: immutable OS, no local data, policy enforcement.
  • Continuity & Recovery: UD Pocket USB boot, Dual Boot, Managed Hypervisor.
  • Delivery Flexibility: VDI/DaaS, PWA, Managed Hypervisor, Enterprise Browser, Local Linux equivalents.
  • Manageability & Integration: UMS, identity, SASE, UEM ecosystem.
  • TCO & Sustainability: lifecycle extension, agentless security, energy & cost savings.
  • Vertical Fit: healthcare, finance, government, retail, manufacturing.
  • Ecosystem & Hardware: IGEL Ready integrations, certified devices.

Stage 2 — Multi-Source Intelligence Gathering

Balanced evidence from product docs, expert evaluations, case studies, and public roadmaps reduces bias and clarifies true feature equivalence vs. marketing claims.

  • Official Documentation: feature lists, architecture diagrams, recovery modes.
  • Expert & Lab Evaluations: benchmarks, analyst notes, POV learnings.
  • User Stories: verified vertical deployments & DX outcomes.
  • Roadmaps & PR: release cadence, strategic shifts, integrations.

Stage 3 — Weighted Comparative Analysis

Scores reflect feature breadth, depth of implementation, integration maturity, and recency of innovation. IGEL is the baseline for capability definitions.

  • Parity checks require technical equivalence (not just naming).
  • Continuity modes (Dual Boot, UD Pocket, Managed Hypervisor) are evaluated for time-to-recover.
  • Ecosystem breadth and certified hardware weigh strongly for scale.
  • Zero-trust posture considers immutability, no local data, and policy control.

Stage 4 — Competitive Capability Map

A side-by-side matrix highlights IGEL advantages (green), parity (yellow), and gaps (red), with vertical accents indicating where each capability is most impactful.

  • Differentiators: Preventative Security Model; layered recovery without external DR tools.
  • Watchlist: areas Unicon promotes (e.g., direct Citrix bundling) for ongoing monitoring.
  • Implications: refine positioning, partner motions, and vertical plays.
